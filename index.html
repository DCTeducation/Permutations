Permutations

Rule: If changing the order changes the outcome, it’s a permutation.

Step 1: Decide before you calculate

Most permutation mistakes happen here.

Before touching a formula, ask:

If I swap the order, does the answer change?

  • Positions or roles? (1st/2nd/3rd, President/Vice, Captain/Assistant) → Permutation
  • Codes or passwords? (PINs, license plates) → Permutation
  • Just choosing a group? (committee, toppings, team members) → Not a permutation

Exam tip: if the question names different jobs or ranks, order matters.

Quick calculator: P(n, r)

Example: P(10,4)=10·9·8·7
